The Vision
Replace the Lawyers! Not Really at all?
Data science can help... Imagine, given a fact pattern and jurisdiction a program can output a probabilistic analysis of the outcome .. Guilty! - 87% probability. This is not science fiction. Data Scientist have the expertise and training to perform just such a feat. To do so scientist need the data. The Internet is filled with well structured legal resources from paid services, free sites that collect links to government sites, and even sites that store and structure those laws for easy access. the Problem is many options are mainly dispersed across many servers or at a cost prohibitive academic research.
Our Solution, Vision and Dream is that the advancements in the fields of a machine learning and predictive analysis (artificial intelligence) can be applied to this data to allow us to further understand and appreciate the laws of this country. Analysis of our laws is an understanding of us. We can invite the data science community, especially those in research or academic programs, to utilize the repository. Data science can focus on using the data and not getting it. The project provides a central consolidated repository for every law and case where data scientist can work and play. We are lawyers with over 15 year of software engineering and court management. Our efforts are focused to collect the requisite data available both as raw bulk download and a specialized repository (in hadoop) to facilitate research and new App development in the law. We do data, the data scientist do the unimaginable.

Support these features
Feature: Federal and State Law Code

Store all the laws for the 50 states, DC, and federal government. This is achieved by collecting and storing the several states already gathered by others. For the other 40 states we are requesting the code via a freedom of information request. In the meantime we are scraping the laws from the various state legislative websites. 40 different scraping programs! This effort requires about 1-2 days per state. Storing the state codes will support others to download and develop ever improving and advancing systems which brings the laws to the people. Legal code supports analysis on how the laws can be interpreted and applied to various fact patterns. Research will progress on the laws themselves including a study on how state laws vary and change over time. Does one state "Lead the way” who are the rebels. How much and what proportion are the laws changing. Your effort can support the effort and disk space to house the laws.

These are our laws

The United States Copyright Office considers edicts of government, such as judicial opinions, administrative rulings, legislative enactments, public ordinances, and similar official legal documents, not copyrightable for reasons of public policy. This applies to such works whether they are Federal, State, or local as well as to those of foreign governments.

Feature: Every Court decision

Store the court decision texts from every Supreme Court, Federal Appellate, and State High Court. Just like lawyers. data scientist use the decisions as the rule-base to interpret the code. Together, the code and cases allow data scientist to fully leverage machine learning. A true legal data science system requires both and having them on the same on-line repository facilitates the research and simplifies the programs.
Cases are generally more available then the code. The US decision provided are by the US government. The challenge is that if the decisions are not available the scraping of these is far more difficult then the code. Also, the Freedom of information which supports public domain for the codes may not be so broad when applied to cases. We are left with scraping the cases and while excluding the copyright protected case annotations. Also, the storage requirement for all the cases is 1000 time that of the laws. It is the decision data that allows machine learning to apply with probable determination the outcome of a specific fact pattern in a specific jurisdiction.

How is the Data Stored and accessed?
Data is stored by document in a nosql (hadoop) database. Generally each court decision is a document. The statutory law of each jurisdiction is divided by title which results in about 100-300 documents each organizing several dozen related laws. An API and data definition is supplied so people can leverage the data for machine learning research. A web enabled demo app will also be provided to demonstrate how to reference the data

A work of the United States government, as defined by United States copyright law, is "a work prepared by an officer or employee of the U.S. federal government as part of that person's official duties."[1] In general, under section 105 of the Copyright Act,[2] such works are not entitled to domestic copyright protection under U.S. law.
